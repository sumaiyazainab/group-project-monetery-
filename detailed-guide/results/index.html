    Mutation Annotation and Variant Activity Analysis

    Mutation Annotation

    Variant sequences are compared to the validated wild-type reference sequence to identify mutations. The system classifies mutations into the following categories:

    • Synonymous mutations — nucleotide changes that do not alter the amino acid sequence
    • Non-synonymous mutations — nucleotide changes that alter the encoded amino acid
    • Truncating mutations — mutations that introduce premature stop codons

    These mutation statistics help researchers understand how sequence variation may influence enzyme function.

    Activity Score

    The Activity Score is a normalised metric that represents the intrinsic catalytic performance of each DNA polymerase variant, independent of expression level or batch variability.

    How it is calculated: Step 1: Activity is calculated by DNA/Protein ratio per variant Step 2: Normalise to per-generation wildtype control mean

    How It Is Calculated

    Step 1 — Raw activity per variant: 

    Raw Activity = DNA_Quantification_fg / Protein_Quantification_pg

    This estimates how much DNA output was produced per unit of protein for each variant.

    Step 2 — Normalise to per-generation wildtype control mean: 

    Activity Score = Raw Activity (variant) / Mean Raw Activity (WT controls, same generation as the variant)

    The per-generation wildtype controls are the rows marked Control = True in the uploaded experimental data file. Each generation's variants are normalised to their own generation's wildtype mean to account for batch effects and assay drift between rounds.

    Activity Score Interpretation

    Score Interpretation
    Above 1.0 Improved catalytic activity relative to wildtype
    ≈ 1.0 Equivalent activity to wildtype
    Below 1.0 Reduced activity relative to wildtype

    For example, an Activity Score of 2.0 means the variant produces twice as much DNA per unit protein as the wildtype control in that generation, suggesting improved polymerase efficiency rather than simply increased expression.

    Biological Justification for the Activity Score

    Raw DNA yield alone does not accurately represent enzymatic performance because variants may differ in protein expression levels. A variant that produces more enzymes could generate more DNA without necessarily having improved catalytic efficiency. By normalising DNA production by protein abundance, the Activity Score estimates the intrinsic catalytic efficiency of the polymerase, independent of expression level.

    Important Notes

    • The Activity Score is a comparative ranking metric — it is designed for ranking and comparing variants within and across generations of the same experiment. Direct comparison across separate experiments should be done with caution.
    • The metric assumes a linear relationship between DNA output and enzymatic activity. Non-linear biological effects are not currently modelled.
