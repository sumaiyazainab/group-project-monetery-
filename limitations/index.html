    Limitations

    The portal is designed to support directed evolution experiments, but the following limitations should be kept in mind when interpreting results:

    Activity Score metric is specific to DNA polymerase engineering: The Activity Score is currently formulated specifically for DNA polymerase directed evolution experiments, where DNA yield is a direct proxy for catalytic activity. The metric assumes that DNA yield reflects polymerase enzymatic activity, and that normalising by protein yield isolates the catalytic efficiency from expression-level differences. This metric would not be appropriate for other types of enzyme or molecule, which would require a different output measurement to reflect catalytic activity. The portal is therefore not currently generalisable to directed evolution experiments targeting other enzyme types.

    Internet connectivity required: The portal requires an active internet connection during experiment staging to retrieve protein sequence and annotation data from the UniProt REST API. Staging cannot be completed in offline environments, and UniProt server unavailability may cause staging to fail. If this occurs, retry again once connectivity is restored.

    Staging alignment threshold: The staging pipeline uses a fixed similarity threshold to validate whether the uploaded plasmid encodes the target protein. In rare cases involving highly divergent or heavily engineered target sequences, the system may not successfully match a valid ORF. If staging fails unexpectedly, verify that the correct UniProt accession ID and plasmid FASTA have been provided.

    Standard genetic code only: The portal currently assumes the standard genetic code during translation. Alternative genetic codes used by specific organisms or organelles are not supported. Results may be unreliable if your expression system uses a non-standard codon table.

    Activity Score is a comparative metric: The Activity Score reflects relative catalytic performance normalised to the wildtype control and is best used for ranking and comparing variants within the same experiment. It is not a mechanistic model of enzyme activity and does not include statistical confidence estimates.

    No replicate support: The system does not currently aggregate replicate measurements or calculate variance and error estimates across replicates. Experimental uploading, and the final results should be interpreted with this in mind when drawing statistical conclusions.

    Mutation classification is sequence-based: Mutations are classified as synonymous, nonsynonymous, truncating, based on sequence comparison alone. Structural effects, protein stability impacts, and domain-level functional consequences are not currently predicted.

    Performance on large datasets: The portal is suited to moderate experimental datasets. Analysis of very large variant libraries may result in increased processing times.
